Brain Reprogramming

Use neuroplasticity to reshape habits, thoughts, and attention — with tiny steps that stick.

“Reprogramming” your brain = working with neuroplasticity, not against it. Below you’ll find plain-English explanations and small, repeatable actions that compound over time.

1) Neuroplasticity — what actually changes

Repeated actions fire networks together — and what fires together, wires together. Over time, useful pathways strengthen and insulate; unused ones fade (pruning). This applies to movement, mood regulation, focus, language, and habits.

  • Repetition: short, frequent reps beat occasional marathons.
  • Emotion & meaning: change sticks when it matters to you.
  • Sleep: consolidation happens most efficiently during sleep.
  • Safety: calmer states (parasympathetic) help learning integrate.
Do now (30s): Name one 60-second behaviour you could repeat daily (e.g., 4–6 breath, one pushup, water one pot).

2) Your habit engine (cue → routine → reward)

Keep the cue and the reward, swap the routine. It’s easier than “willpower”.

Map a loop

Cue: mid-afternoon dip. Routine: mindless scroll. Reward: stimulation.

Swap: 20 wall pushups + water → same reward + mini win.

Tiny Habits

Anchor a 30–60s action to something you already do.

Example: After the kettle clicks, water one pot. Celebrate micro-wins.

If–Then plans

If (situation), then (tiny next step) to reduce friction.

Example: If I feel overwhelmed, then I breathe 4–6 for one minute.

3) Thought tools that reshape pathways

Thought reframing

Notice a sticky thought → check evidence → craft a balanced alternative (accuracy, not forced positivity).

  • “I always fail” → “I sometimes struggle, and I finished X and Y.”
  • “It must be perfect” → “Done at 80% beats stuck at 0%.”

See Thought Reframing for cards and examples.

Affirmations that work

Believable, present-tense, and tied to action. They prime attention to notice matching evidence.

  • “I can take one step now.” → start a 2-minute task.
  • “I fuel my body kindly.” → pour a glass of water.

See Affirmations for a starter set.

Exposure, gently

Create a graded ladder (easy → moderate → hard). Pair exposures with calm breathing; stop around 5–6/10, let the wave pass, repeat another day.

4) State management (fight/flight, rest/digest, shutdown)

Change sticks best when your nervous system is steady. Use small switches to nudge toward calm or gentle activation.

Down-shift (too revved)

  • 4–6 breathing × 2 minutes
  • Long exhale sighs; unclench jaw; drop shoulders
  • Warm drink; dim lights; slower music

Up-shift (too flat)

  • 60–120s brisk walk or stairs
  • Cool water splash; morning daylight
  • Upbeat track + simple movement

5) Environment & identity (make the good path easy)

  • Prompt the cue: dumbbells by the kettle; watering can by the door.
  • Remove friction: pre-chopped veg; seeds + compost ready in a tray.
  • Identity questions: “What would a person-who-trains do next?”
  • Streaks & tracking: tick a calendar; visible wins motivate.
  • Accountability: text a friend after your 2-minute action.

6) Lifestyle builders that amplify change

Sleep

Consistent wake time; morning light; reduce late caffeine; wind-down; phone out of reach.

Movement

Daily “minimums” (10–20 minutes); sprinkle 30–60s “movement snacks”.

Nutrition

Protein + fibre each meal; hydration habit (glass per meal); batch a simple base (beans/rice/soup).

Sunlight & connection

Morning daylight; brief nature; small, regular check-ins with safe people.

7) Notes for ADHD & Autism

ADHD

  • Body-doubling, visual timers, 2-minute starts.
  • Make step one ridiculously small; use novelty.
  • Externalise: checklists, calendar blocks, pinned tabs.

Autism

  • Prioritise sensory comfort; reduce transitions.
  • Use written plans and simple scripts for tricky moments.
  • Protect decompression after high-demand events.

8) Troubleshooting & relapse plan

  • Too big? Halve it until it fits 60 seconds.
  • Forgetting? Attach to a strong anchor (kettle, door, calendar alert).
  • Bored? Change soundtrack/location/tool — keep the cue/time.
  • Bad week? Switch to “minimum viable habit”: one rep, one breath, one page.
  • Relapse script: “I’m restarting today with one tiny step.”

9) Starter kit for the next 7 days

  1. Choose one loop: Kettle cue → 4–6 breathing (60–120s) → Reward: calmer start.
  2. One tiny action: “Open the note and write one ugly sentence.”
  3. Emergency phrase: “Lower the bar. One minute counts.”
  4. Track: draw 7 boxes; tick daily; avoid two misses in a row.
