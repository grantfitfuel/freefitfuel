Yoga & Meditation

Wind down your nervous system with slow nasal breathing, longer exhales, gentle holds and floor-based poses to support deep relaxation and lower stress load.

Pick a quick reset (5–10 min), an evening unwind (20–30 min), or a deep restore (45–60 min). Use the metronome, calm sounds and your own guided audio.

Quick Resets (5–10 min)

Desk Decompress ~6–8 min

  1. Box Breathing — 4-4-4-4 for 12 cycles.
  2. Seated Neck & Shoulder Melt — circles + ear-to-shoulder.
  3. Standing Forward Fold — soft knees, 60–90s.

Floor Unknot ~8–10 min

  1. Constructive Rest — 2–3 min.
  2. Supine Twist — 60–90s/side.
  3. Crocodile Breathing — 3–4 min.

Evening Unwind (20–30 min)

Gentle Yin Flow ~25 min

  1. Child’s Pose 3 min.
  2. Cat–Cow 1–2 min.
  3. Low Lunge 2 min/side.
  4. Wide-Leg Seated Fold 3–4 min.
  5. Supported Bridge 3 min.
  6. Reclined Twist 2 min/side.
  7. Legs-Up-the-Wall 5–7 min.
  8. Savasana 2–3 min.

Deep Restore (45–60 min)

Restorative Set ~50–60 min

  1. Coherent Breathing 5–7 min.
  2. Supported Child’s Pose 6–8 min.
  3. Reclined Bound Angle 8–10 min.
  4. Side-lying Opener 6–8 min/side.
  5. Legs-Up-on-Chair 8–10 min.
  6. Yoga Nidra 10–12 min.

Safety Notes

  • Stop if dizzy or sharp pain.
  • Pregnancy/low BP: avoid long holds.
  • Comfort first: use props liberally.

Important Note

These practices support relaxation and general wellbeing; they are not a substitute for medical care. If you have health conditions, consult a qualified professional. Stop any practice that causes pain or distress.
