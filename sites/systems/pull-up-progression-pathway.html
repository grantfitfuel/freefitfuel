Pull-Up Progression Pathway

A structured pull-up progression system focused on scapular control, lat activation, assisted progressions, grip strength and sustainable full pull-up development.

Strength & skill All levels 2–3 times weekly Scalable progression

What This System Is For

Many people fail pull-ups because they jump too quickly to full reps without building scapular control, grip endurance and movement confidence first. This pathway breaks the process into manageable stages that can scale from complete beginner through to stronger weighted work.

Best For

  • Calisthenics foundations
  • Upper-body strength
  • Grip improvement

Phase 1 — Scapular Control & Hanging Confidence

Dead Hangs

3 × 15–30 seconds

  • Use a comfortable overhand grip.
  • Keep shoulders controlled rather than collapsing.
  • Build grip confidence gradually.

Scapular Pulls

3 × 6–10

  • Stay mostly straight-armed.
  • Pull the shoulder blades down gently.
  • Focus on control rather than height.

Band Lat Activation

3 × 12–15

  • Use light resistance.
  • Drive elbows towards the ribs.
  • Pause briefly at contraction.

Phase 2 — Assisted Pull-Up Strength

Band-Assisted Pull-Ups

4 × 4–8

  • Use enough support to maintain good form.
  • Pull the chest towards the bar.
  • Control the lowering phase.

Feet-Assisted Pull-Ups

3 × 6–10

  • Use a box or bench for support.
  • Reduce assistance over time.
  • Keep the movement smooth.

Inverted Rows

3 × 8–12

  • Keep the chest lifted.
  • Drive elbows backwards.
  • Maintain body tension.

Phase 3 — Negative Strength & Full Pull-Ups

Negative Pull-Ups

3 × 3–5

  • Start at the top position.
  • Lower slowly over 3–5 seconds.
  • Keep shoulders controlled throughout.

Single Pull-Up Attempts

3–5 quality attempts

  • Do not grind ugly reps.
  • Focus on smooth controlled pulling.
  • Rest adequately between attempts.

Pull-Up Clusters

Multiple low-rep sets

  • Accumulate quality reps gradually.
  • Avoid complete fatigue.
  • Keep movement standards high.

Technique Principles

Progress by quality, not ego. Better scapular control and cleaner reps matter more than chasing sloppy numbers.

Helpful Cues

  • Pull elbows towards the ribs.
  • Keep the chest proud.
  • Control the lowering phase.
  • Stay tight through the core.

Common Errors

  • Jerking violently from the bottom.
  • Collapsing shoulders.
  • Excessive swinging.
  • Ignoring elbow pain signals.
