Yoga & Meditation

Wind down your nervous system. Slow nasal breathing, longer exhales, and floor-based shapes. Guided relaxations help you shut out the noise and sink into real rest.

Quick Resets (5–10 min)

Desk Decompress ~6–8 min

  1. Box Breathing — 4–4–4–4 for 12 cycles.
  2. Seated neck & shoulder circles — 2×20s/side.
  3. Standing Forward Fold (Uttanasana) — soft knees 60–90s.

Exhale a touch longer than inhale; jaw and tongue soft.

Floor Unknot ~8–10 min

  1. Constructive Rest — 2–3 min.
  2. Reclined Twist — 60–90s/side.
  3. Crocodile Breathing — low belly breaths 3–4 min.

Evening Unwind (20–30 min)

Gentle Yin Flow ~25 min

  1. Child’s Pose (Balasana) — 3 min (bolster under chest).
  2. Cat–Cow — 1–2 min slow.
  3. Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana) — 2 min/side with blocks.
  4. Wide-Leg Seated Fold (Upavistha Konasana) — 3–4 min.
  5. Supported Bridge — 3 min.
  6. Reclined Twist — 2 min/side.
  7. Legs-Up-the-Wall (Viparita Karani) — 5–7 min.
  8. Savasana — 2–3 min.

Breath-Led Unwind ~20–22 min

  1. 4-7-8 breathing — 10 cycles.
  2. Supported Forward Fold — 3 min.
  3. Supine Hamstring (strap) — 2 min/side.
  4. Box breathing — 6–8 cycles.
  5. Body-scan in Savasana — 5–6 min.

Read-Along Scripts

Safe Space (matches Safe Space Visualisation)

[Settle] Lie or sit comfortably. Imagine a door that opens into a place where you feel completely safe. As you breathe out, the door opens a little more… and the noise of the world turns down like a radio.

[Arrive] Step inside. Notice colours, light, textures. A chair, a window, a view. Each breath out softens your shoulders and jaw. Each breath in brings a sense of “I belong here.”

[Deepen] Find a spot to rest. Maybe a blanket, a warm mug in your hands. The air is the perfect temperature. With every exhale, your body settles 5% deeper.

[Close] When you’re ready, leave the door slightly ajar. You can return anytime. Let your breath be natural. Open your eyes softly.

Candle Flame (matches Candle Flame Focus)

[Settle] Picture a candle in front of you. The room is dim. With each inhale the flame lifts; with each exhale it steadies.

[Gaze] Notice the base of the flame, the warmer orange core, the almost-invisible blue. Thoughts that arrive drift out with the smoke. The flame remains.

[Count] Quietly count ten breaths: in-one… out-one… in-two… out-two… If you lose count, begin again at one—gently.

[Close] Let the image fade like an afterglow behind your eyelids. Keep the steadiness you found.

Floating Scan (matches Floating Body Scan)

[Settle] Imagine you’re supported by warm, still water. The back of your body floats; the water holds all of you.

[Scan] Crown of the head soft… forehead wide… jaw unhooks. Shoulders float wider. Arms drift heavy and warm. Chest expands on the inhale, settles on the exhale.

[Lower body] Belly soft. Hips heavy and supported. Thighs, knees, calves release. Feet warm and loose. From head to toes, you’re buoyant and safe.

[Close] The water becomes a soft surface beneath you. Keep the ease as you return.

Whispered Countdown (matches Whispered Countdown)

[Settle] With each exhale, imagine a dimmer switch turning the world down one notch.

[Count] In your mind, count slowly from twenty to one. With twenty, your shoulders drop. Nineteen, your face unknots. Eighteen, your belly softens. Keep going, one number per breath. At ten, your whole body feels heavier. At five, the breath is quiet and smooth. One… you’re resting, safe.

[Close] Let the numbers disappear like footprints in tidewater. Linger, or return gently.

Why these help

  • Longer exhales nudge the parasympathetic system (rest/digest).
  • Imagery narrows attention and down-shifts threat detection.
  • Supported positions reduce muscular effort → brain reads “safe”.

Important Note

These practices support relaxation and general wellbeing; they are not a substitute for medical care. If you have health conditions, injuries, or mental health concerns, consult a qualified professional. Stop any practice that causes pain, dizziness, or distress.
