Neuroscience (practical)

Simple, evidence-aligned tools to help your nervous system settle, focus, and recover.

Physiological sigh (1 minute)

Two short inhales through the nose, one long exhale through the mouth. Repeat 3–5 times to lower acute stress.

Box breathing (2–4 minutes)

Inhale 4 — hold 4 — exhale 4 — hold 4. A steady pattern helps shift toward calm and focus.

Grounding scan

Notice 5 things you can see, 4 you can feel, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. Brings attention to the present.

Light, movement, sleep

Morning daylight, a brief afternoon walk, and a consistent sleep window stabilise mood and energy.

When stress spikes

Stand, shake out limbs for 20–30 seconds, and lengthen your exhale. Then take a slow lap or stretch your calves against a wall.

Notes

These are general strategies. Adapt to your body and speak to a clinician if you have medical concerns.
