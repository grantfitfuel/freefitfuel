Carer Support

Caring is meaningful and demanding. These notes aim to reduce overwhelm, protect your energy, and keep connection at the centre.

What you can control

Small levers matter: routine meals, hydration, short movement, and 10–15 minute rest windows. Reduce decision load where you can.

Boundaries that protect care

Clear “office hours”, shared calendars, and a simple handover list make support from others easier and reduce burnout.

Staying connected

Schedule brief check-ins with a friend or sibling. Consider group chats for updates so you’re not repeating the same message.

Anticipatory grief

It’s grief that starts before loss. Naming it helps. See our short guide under Mental Health for practical ways to cope.

Read the guide

Respite & practical help

List specific tasks others can take (shopping list, prescriptions, laundry). Share it when people ask “How can I help?”

Emergency contacts

Keep key numbers in one place (GP, out-of-hours, pharmacy, neighbour). Put a printed copy by the door.
