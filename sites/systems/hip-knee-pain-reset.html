Hip & Knee Pain Reset

A lower-body reset system for hip stiffness, knee irritation, cramp, walking discomfort and pain that worsens after inactivity.

Low-to-moderate intensity Movement restoration Walking support Recovery aware

What This System Is For

Hip and knee discomfort often becomes worse when movement drops, stiffness builds up and tissues lose confidence under load. This system focuses on controlled movement, circulation, mobility and gradual rebuilding rather than aggressive pushing.

Common Signs

  • Pain after sitting still
  • Hip stiffness when moving again
  • Walking discomfort
  • Deep aching around the hip or knee
  • Cramp and tightness

Can Pair With

  • Knee Capacity Reset
  • Fascia Flow Reset
  • Lymphatic Recovery Flow
  • Lower-Leg Stability System
  • Walking programmes

Seek Medical Advice If

  • Severe swelling develops
  • The joint locks or gives way repeatedly
  • Night pain becomes severe
  • Rapid weakness develops
  • You cannot weight-bear properly

Phase 1 — Restore Comfortable Movement

The goal is confidence under movement. Gentle movement usually beats complete inactivity for stiffness-driven pain.

Hip Circles

2 × 30–45 seconds

  • Move slowly and smoothly.
  • Use support if needed.
  • Keep the knees soft.

Supported Sit-to-Stand

2–3 × 6–10

  • Use a chair or bench.
  • Control the lowering phase.
  • Push through the floor smoothly.

Gentle Walking Blocks

5–10 minutes

  • Keep pace comfortable.
  • Use shorter bouts if needed.
  • Focus on movement consistency.

Phase 2 — Build Lower-Body Capacity

Step-Ups

2–3 × 6–10 each side

  • Use a low step initially.
  • Control the descent.
  • Do not rush the movement.

Bridge Variations

2–3 × 8–12

  • Drive through the heels.
  • Keep the ribs controlled.
  • Pause briefly at the top.

Calf Raises

2–3 × 12–15

  • Rise and lower slowly.
  • Use support if necessary.
  • Do not bounce aggressively.

Phase 3 — Return To More Normal Training

Controlled Squat Patterns

3 × 6–10

  • Use manageable depth.
  • Keep knees tracking cleanly.
  • Progress gradually.

Walking Tolerance Progression

Steady increases

  • Add distance gradually.
  • Monitor next-day response.
  • Consistency matters more than speed.

Light Power Reintroduction

Only if comfortable

  • Mini pogo hops
  • Fast marching
  • Low-level dynamic movement

Recovery Principles

Helpful Approaches

  • Frequent gentle movement
  • Hydration and sleep support
  • Gradual walking exposure
  • Mobility before long sitting periods

Often Unhelpful

  • Sudden huge activity spikes
  • Complete movement avoidance
  • Grinding through sharp pain
  • Ignoring recovery quality
