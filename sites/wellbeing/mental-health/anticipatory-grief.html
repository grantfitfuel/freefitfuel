Anticipatory Grief

Grief can start before a loss — especially with long illness or dementia. Naming it reduces the “why am I like this?” loop.

What it is

Sadness, anger, guilt, relief, and numbness can all show up. They may cycle. None of them mean you care less.

Anchor points

Micro-routines: morning light, cup of tea outside, brief walk, 2-minute breathing. Tiny anchors help days feel workable.

Connection

One update channel for family/friends reduces repeats. Accept practical help; give people specific tasks they can choose from.

When it’s heavy

If you feel stuck or unsafe, see the Crisis page for urgent support options and speak to a clinician.
