What we’re doing (and why)

In 12 weeks you’ll develop reliable movement patterns, aerobic capacity that makes everything easier, and recovery habits that keep progress rolling. We’ll progress via volume → tempo → load, so joints and tendons adapt before intensity climbs.

Strength Focus

Push • Pull • Squat • Hinge • Carry • Core

Start where form is crisp; add reps before load.

Endurance Focus

Zone-2 base + short intervals

Talkable pace most days. One spicy day as tolerance allows.

Recovery Focus

Breath, sleep, steps, hydration

Calm the system → better adaptation → fewer stalls.

Progression by Month

  • Weeks 1–4: Learn form. Same lifts, higher quality reps.
  • Weeks 5–8: Add a set or slow negatives (tempo 3–1–2).
  • Weeks 9–12: Modest load increase or tougher variants.

Deload if joints bark: cut total volume by ~30% for one week.

Cardio & Conditioning

Zone-2 (talkable pace)

  • 2–3× / week • 20–60 min — walk/jog, bike, row, hike.
  • Breathing through the nose as much as possible; you should be able to speak full sentences.

Gentle Intervals (optional 1× / week)

  • 6–10 × 60s steady / 60–90s easy
  • Keep form light; finish feeling you could do 2 more.

Carries & Steps (daily life conditioning)

  • 1–2 intentional carry blocks (farmer’s/suitcase) on strength days.
  • Steps goal: a level you can repeat (e.g., 6–9k).

Mobility & Breath (5–15 min daily)

Reset Flow

  • 90/90 hip switches × 6–10/side
  • Cat–cow × 8–12
  • Half-kneeling ankle rocks × 10/side

Soft Tissue

  • Calves / lats / glutes — 60–90s each
  • “Just enough” pressure to relax

Breath Downshift

  • Coherent breathing 5/5 or 4/6 — 3–5 min
  • Evening: dim lights, longer exhales

Boss Fight — Foundation Trial (Week 12)

Warm up 8–10 min. Form first. Record times & loads — this becomes your checkpoint for Phase II.
