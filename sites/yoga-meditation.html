Yoga & Meditation

Wind down your nervous system. Practices emphasise slow nasal breathing, longer exhales, gentle holds and floor-based poses to support deep relaxation and lower stress load.

Pick a quick reset (5–10 min), an evening unwind (20–30 min), or a deep restore (45–60 min). Use the metronome, calm sounds and your own guided audio.

Quick Resets (5–10 min)

Desk Decompress ~6–8 min

  1. Box Breathing — 4-4-4-4 for 12 cycles (≈4–5 min).
  2. Seated Neck & Shoulder Melt — circles + ear-to-shoulder 2×20s/side.
  3. Standing Forward Fold (Uttanasana) — soft knees 60–90s.

Silent, nasal breathing. Let exhales be a touch longer.

Floor Unknot ~8–10 min

  1. Constructive Rest (on back) 2–3 min.
  2. Supine/Reclined Twist 60–90s/side, support knees.
  3. Crocodile Breathing (prone) 3–4 min of low belly breaths.

Evening Unwind (20–30 min)

Gentle Yin Flow ~25 min

  1. Child’s Pose (Balasana) 3 min (bolster under chest).
  2. Cat–Cow 1–2 min slow.
  3. Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana) 2 min/side with blocks.
  4. Dragonfly / Wide-Leg Seated Fold (Upavistha Konasana) 3–4 min.
  5. Supported Bridge 3 min.
  6. Reclined Twist 2 min/side.
  7. Legs-Up-the-Wall (Viparita Karani) 5–7 min.
  8. Savasana 2–3 min.
Props & cues
  • Comfort over stretch; add cushions until the shape feels easy.
  • Exhale longer: in 4, out 6–8; soften jaw and tongue.
  • Dim light; phone on Do Not Disturb.

Breath-Led Unwind ~20–22 min

  1. 4-7-8 breathing — 10 cycles (≈4–5 min).
  2. Supported Forward Fold 3 min.
  3. Supine Hamstring Strap (Supta Padangusthasana) 2 min/side.
  4. Box breathing — 6–8 cycles (≈3–4 min).
  5. Savasana body-scan 5–6 min.

Deep Restore (45–60 min)

Restorative Set-Up ~50–60 min

  1. Coherent breathing (in 5, out 5) 5–7 min.
  2. Supported Child’s Pose 6–8 min.
  3. Reclined Bound Angle (Supta Baddha Konasana) 8–10 min.
  4. Side-lying Heart Opener (restorative) 6–8 min/side.
  5. Legs-Up-on-Chair 8–10 min.
  6. Yoga Nidra (guided rest) 10–12 min.

Guided Scripts (read aloud or record)

Coherent Breathing — Script (5–7 min)

Goal: smooth nasal breathing at ~5 breaths/min. Use a calm, unhurried tone. Pause between lines.

[Set-up] Find a comfortable position—lying down or seated, back supported. Soften the jaw and tongue. Let the eyes be heavy or closed.

[Arrival] Notice where your body touches the floor or chair. Shoulders soften. Belly soft.

[Pattern] We’ll breathe in for 5, out for 5. All through the nose. If that feels too long, use 4–4; if it’s easy, try 6–6 another day.

[Minute 1] In…2…3…4…5. Out…2…3…4…5. In…2…3…4…5. Out…2…3…4…5.

[Minute 2] Smooth inhale…2…3…4…5. Soften on the exhale…2…3…4…5. Let the breath be quiet. Shoulders heavy.

[Minute 3] In…2…3…4…5. Out…2…3…4…5. The belly widens slightly on the inhale, settles on the exhale.

[Minute 4] In…2…3…4…5. Out…2…3…4…5. Imagine the exhale releasing the day’s effort.

[Minute 5] In…2…3…4…5. Out…2…3…4…5. Keep it easy, like waves arriving and leaving.

[Optional 1–2 min] Continue this gentle rhythm if it feels good.

[Close] Let the count fade. Breathe naturally. Notice how the body feels now. When you’re ready, let light back in.

Body-Scan — Script (10–12 min)

Goal: invite ease and safety signals. Speak slowly, with soft pauses. Props so the body can truly rest.

[Set-up] Lie comfortably on your back or side. Support knees and head as needed. Hands rest where they feel at ease. Close the eyes if comfortable.

[Arrival] Soft inhale through the nose… long easy exhale. Feel the weight of your body being held. Nothing to fix; nothing to achieve.

[Head & face] Soften the forehead; the space between the eyebrows widens. Unclench the jaw. Tongue rests lightly. Lips un-grip.

[Neck & shoulders] Notice the length of the back of the neck. Shoulders heavy—front, sides, back softening into support.

[Right arm & hand] Sense upper arm… elbow… forearm… wrist… palm… each finger: thumb, index, middle, ring, little. Warmth or tingling is fine; or no sensation is fine too.

[Left arm & hand] Upper arm… elbow… forearm… wrist… palm… fingers. Hands effortlessly supported.

[Chest & back] Breath moves the ribs: widen on inhale, settle on exhale. Back of the ribs spreading gently into the surface beneath you.

[Abdomen & diaphragm] Belly soft. Breath low and wide—no effort. If helpful, rest a hand on the belly and feel it rise and fall.

[Pelvis & hips] Notice the weight of the pelvis. If there’s gripping, invite 5% less effort. Hips heavy and supported.

[Right leg & foot] Thigh… knee… calf… ankle… heel… sole… top of the foot… each toe. The whole leg is heavy.

[Left leg & foot] Thigh… knee… calf… ankle… heel… sole… top of the foot… toes. Both legs at rest.

[Whole body] Sense the body all at once—head to toes—held by the ground. Breathe in softly… and out a little longer. Let the breath become effortless.

[Close] Linger if you wish. Otherwise, deepen the breath, invite small movements, roll to one side, and come up slowly.

Why this lowers stress

Nervous system down-shift

  • Longer exhales and brief holds increase vagal tone, steering toward “rest & digest”.
  • Supported shapes reduce effort → signal safety → lower sympathetic drive.
  • Dim light, warmth, and eye cover reduce alerting input.

Practical cortisol hygiene

  • Place calming sessions after stressful blocks or in the evening.
  • Avoid high-intensity work right before bed; choose restorative or breathwork instead.
  • Keep caffeine earlier; silence notifications during sessions.
Safety
  • Light-headed? Shorten holds or breathe normally.
  • Pregnancy/low blood pressure: avoid long breath holds; prefer coherent breathing.
  • Sharp pain ≠ normal: add props or skip the pose.

Important Note

These practices support relaxation and general wellbeing; they are not a substitute for medical care. If you have health conditions, injuries, or mental health concerns, consult a qualified professional. Stop any practice that causes pain, dizziness, or distress.
