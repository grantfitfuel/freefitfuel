Getting started

What you need

  • Mat or carpeted space
  • Optional: pair of dumbbells (light/medium), resistance band
  • Timer (phone) and water

No kit? Use the bodyweight versions in each plan.

How to progress

  • When all sets feel solid, add +1–2 reps or +1 set
  • Or increase load by the smallest step (0.5–2 kg)
  • Keep reps in reserve: stop with ~1–2 reps left in the tank

Consistency beats perfection. Aim for 2–4 sessions/week.

Warm-up (6–8 minutes)

Flow

  1. Neck/shoulder rolls × 8
  2. Arm swings & thoracic rotations × 8
  3. Hip circles × 8 each direction
  4. World’s greatest stretch × 4 each side
  5. Glute bridge × 10
  6. March in place × 30–45 s

Breathe slow; exhale on the effort.

Beginner Plan 2–3× / week • ~20–25 min

Full-body A

1) Sit-to-Stand 3×8–12 (slow lower)

2) Incline Push-up 3×6–10 (wall/counter)

3) Hip Hinge 3×8–12 (hands on thighs)

4) Dead Bug 3×6–10 / side

5) March & Balance 2×30–45s

Rest 45–60s. If breathless, extend rest; quality over speed.

Full-body B (optional swap)

1) Split-Squat (assisted) 3×6–10 / leg

2) Band Row 3×10–12

3) Wall Press 3×10–12

4) Glute Bridge 3×10–15

5) Farmer Carry (light) 2×20–40m

Alternate A/B across the week. Keep 1–2 reps in reserve.

Bodyweight only? Use wall/counter variations and bands. Add dumbbells later for progressive overload.

Intermediate Plan 3× / week • ~30–40 min

Push–Pull–Legs (Day 1)

1) Push-up (knees/high) 4×6–10

2) DB Row 4×8–12 / side

3) Goblet Squat 4×8–12

4) DB Overhead Press 3×8–10

5) Side Plank 3×20–40s / side

Hinge–Carry–Core (Day 2)

1) Romanian Deadlift (DB) 4×8–12

2) Split Squat 3×8–10 / leg

3) Band Pulldown / Door-row 4×10–12

4) Farmer Carry (moderate) 3×40–60m

5) Dead Bug / Hollow Hold 3×8–12 or 20–40s

Progression: when top reps are solid, add load or one extra set.

Advanced Plan 3–4× / week • ~40–55 min

Upper (Strength bias)

1) Push-up / Weighted 5×5–8

2) Single-arm Row (heavier) 5×6–8 / side

3) DB Overhead Press 4×6–8

4) Chest-supported Row 4×8–10

5) Plank Variations 3×40–60s

Lower (Strength + power)

1) Goblet Squat (heavy) 5×5–8

2) RDL (heavy) 4×6–8

3) Split Squat 4×8–10 / leg

4) Hip Thrust / Bridge 4×8–12

5) Farmer or Rack Carry 3×60–80m

Conditioning finisher (optional): 6–10 minutes of intervals (20s brisk / 40s easy) on a walk, step, or bike.

Mobility & recovery

Post-session cool-down (4–6 min)

  • Calf stretch (wall) 2×20–30s / side
  • Hip flexor stretch (half-kneel) 2×20–30s / side
  • Child’s pose / thoracic rotations × 6–8
  • Box breathing 1–2 minutes (inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4)

Active recovery days

  • 20–40 min easy walk or swim
  • Light mobility flow or yoga
  • Tai Chi / Qi Gong for balance & calm

Form cues (quick wins)

Hinge & Squat

  • Hinge: “Push hips back, soft knees, long spine.”
  • Squat: “Knees track over mid-feet, chest proud, full foot on floor.”
  • Depth: Pain-free range > depth at any cost.

Push & Pull

  • Push: Wrists stacked, ribs down, exhale on press.
  • Pull: Start with shoulder blade; squeeze, then arm.
  • Tempo: 2s up, 2–3s down (own the negative).

Safety

OK: normal muscle effort, light burn, breathing faster.

Caution: sharp joint pain, dizziness, chest tightness — stop, rest, assess.

Urgent: chest pain, severe breathlessness, fainting — seek medical help.

New or multiple conditions? Check with your clinician. Progress gradually. Hydrate and sleep.

FAQs

How long until I notice changes?

Often 2–3 weeks for energy and mood; 4–8 weeks for visible strength and shape, assuming 2–4 sessions per week.

What if I have no dumbbells?

Start with bodyweight and bands. Add load later for steady progression.

How sore is too sore?

Light next-day muscle soreness is normal. Joint pain or sharp pain is not — scale back or change the movement.

Can I mix cardio with these plans?

Yes: walk most days; add brisk intervals 1–2× weekly. Keep at least one full rest day.
