Fascia Flow Reset

A gentle whole-body movement system for stiffness, deskbound recovery, posture, breathing and better movement quality. Use it as a daily reset, warm-up, cool-down or low-energy recovery option.

15 minutes Low intensity All levels Daily or after long sitting

What This System Is For

Fascia Flow Reset is not a fat-loss shortcut or a magic detox. It is a practical movement flow designed to help your body feel less stuck. It combines gentle joint circles, long-chain mobility, controlled breathing and low-impact movement so you can move better before harder training or recover better afterwards.

Best For

  • Deskbound stiffness
  • Morning tightness
  • Recovery days
  • Warm-ups before strength work
  • Low-energy movement days

  • Numbness or weakness develops

The 15-Minute Flow

Move slowly enough that your breathing can stay calm. The aim is not to stretch aggressively. The aim is to remind the body that movement is safe, available and repeatable.

1. Standing Breath Reset

1 minute

  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Breathe in through the nose if comfortable.
  • Let the ribs expand gently, then exhale slowly.
  • Keep shoulders relaxed and jaw unclenched.

2. Neck, Shoulder and Wrist Circles

2 minutes

  • Use small, slow circles rather than forcing range.
  • Circle shoulders forwards and backwards.
  • Open and close the hands, then circle the wrists.
  • Keep movement smooth and pain-free.

3. Spinal Wave

2 minutes

  • Stand with soft knees and hands resting lightly on thighs.
  • Round the upper back gently, then lengthen through the chest.
  • Move like a slow wave rather than a hard stretch.
  • Keep the lower back comfortable.

4. Side Reach and Rib Glide

2 minutes

  • Reach one arm overhead and gently lean to the opposite side.
  • Keep both feet grounded.
  • Move in and out of the position instead of holding hard.
  • Repeat evenly on both sides.

5. Hip Circles and Figure-Eight Hips

2 minutes

  • Place hands on hips or hold a chair for support.
  • Circle the hips slowly in both directions.
  • Then draw a small figure-eight shape with the pelvis.
  • Keep knees soft and breathing steady.

6. Hamstring Sweep to Reach

2 minutes

  • Step one heel forward with toes lifted.
  • Hinge slightly at the hips and sweep the hands down the front leg.
  • Return to standing and reach gently overhead.
  • Alternate sides without bouncing.

7. Ankle Rockers and Calf Pump

2 minutes

  • Rock from heels to toes slowly.
  • Then perform gentle calf raises.
  • Hold a wall or chair if needed.
  • Keep the movement quiet and controlled.

8. Slow Walkout or March Finish

2 minutes

  • Either march gently on the spot or walk around the room.
  • Let the arms swing naturally.
  • Finish with three slow breaths.
  • Notice whether movement feels easier than when you started.

Progression Rules

Progress by smoothness before intensity. If the movement feels calmer, easier and more coordinated, the session has done its job.

Make It Easier

  • Do 6 to 8 minutes only
  • Use a chair or wall for support
  • Reduce range of motion
  • Move seated for the upper-body sections

Make It Stronger

  • Add a second round
  • Slow each movement down
  • Add light bands for shoulder work
  • Use it before a strength or conditioning session
