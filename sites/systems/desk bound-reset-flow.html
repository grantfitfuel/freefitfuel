Deskbound Reset Flow

A fully coached movement-break system for long sitting days, posture reset, circulation, neck stiffness, hip tightness and deskbound fatigue.

Deskbound support Low impact Posture reset Circulation support

What This System Is For

Long periods of sitting can increase stiffness, reduce circulation, tighten the hips and chest, and leave the body feeling heavy or disconnected. This system is designed to break up sedentary periods without turning the reset into another exhausting task.

Move little and often. Five calm minutes repeated during the day usually works better than waiting until the body feels completely locked up.

Useful For

  • Long office hours
  • Gaming or studying sessions
  • Remote working
  • Hip tightness
  • Neck and shoulder tension

Pairs Well With

  • Daily Recovery Flow
  • Joint Control Foundations
  • Walking breaks
  • Tai chi walking
  • Lower-leg stability work

Reduce Intensity If

  • Dizziness develops
  • Back pain sharpens
  • Numbness increases
  • Balance feels unsafe
  • Symptoms worsen after movement

How To Use This Flow

Mini Breaks

Use 3–5 minute resets every 45–90 minutes during long sitting periods.

After Work

Use a longer calmer round to reduce accumulated stiffness and decompress.

Low Energy Days

Prioritise circulation and posture rather than intensity or sweating.

Phase 1 — Wake The Body Back Up

Standing Posture Reset

30–60 seconds

Purpose

Helps reverse the collapsed posture many people drift into during long sitting periods.

Starting Position
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Keep knees soft.
  • Relax the jaw and shoulders.
How To Do It
  • Lengthen gently through the crown of the head.
  • Open the chest slightly.
  • Let the shoulders settle down naturally.
  • Breathe calmly.
What You Should Feel

A lighter more open posture rather than rigid military tension.

Common Mistakes
  • Overarching the lower back.
  • Forcing the chest out aggressively.
  • Tensing shoulders upwards.
  • Holding breath.
Easier / Harder

Easier: lean lightly against a wall.
Harder: hold the position while breathing slowly for longer.

Neck & Shoulder Decompression

1–2 minutes

Purpose

Reduces tension and stiffness around the neck, upper traps and shoulders.

How To Do It
  • Turn head slowly side to side.
  • Roll shoulders gently forwards and backwards.
  • Open and close the chest slowly.
  • Keep movements relaxed and smooth.
What You Should Feel

Gentle circulation and reduced tension. Not aggressive stretching discomfort.

Common Mistakes
  • Rolling the neck aggressively.
  • Moving too quickly.
  • Forcing range.
  • Shrugging shoulders upwards.
Easier / Harder

Easier: smaller movements.
Harder: add short pauses in comfortable positions.

Breathing Reset

1–2 minutes

Purpose

Helps interrupt shallow stress breathing patterns that often build during focused desk work.

How To Do It
  • Breathe slowly through the nose if comfortable.
  • Allow ribs to expand gently.
  • Keep shoulders relaxed.
  • Exhale slowly without forcing.
What You Should Feel

Reduced tension and calmer breathing rhythm.

Common Mistakes
  • Overbreathing aggressively.
  • Trying too hard to relax.
  • Raising shoulders excessively.
  • Forcing huge breaths.
Easier / Harder

Easier: shorter breathing cycles.
Harder: extend the exhale gently.

Phase 2 — Restore Mobility & Circulation

Hip Mobility Flow

1–2 minutes

Purpose

Helps reduce hip stiffness that builds during prolonged sitting.

How To Do It
  • Circle hips slowly in both directions.
  • Shift weight gently side to side.
  • Keep knees soft.
  • Use support if balance is uncertain.
What You Should Feel

Gentle mobility and circulation through hips and pelvis.

Common Mistakes
  • Locking knees.
  • Forcing large circles.
  • Moving too fast.
  • Ignoring balance issues.
Easier / Harder

Easier: smaller ranges.
Harder: slow the movement and add pauses.

Slow Marching

2–3 minutes

Purpose

Restores circulation and rhythm after prolonged inactivity.

How To Do It
  • March slowly on the spot.
  • Place feet quietly.
  • Coordinate opposite arm and leg.
  • Stay relaxed.
What You Should Feel

Gentle warmth and increased circulation. Not hard cardio effort.

Common Mistakes
  • Stomping heavily.
  • Rushing.
  • Tensing shoulders.
  • Trying to burn calories aggressively.
Easier / Harder

Easier: hold support.
Harder: increase duration gradually.

Calf & Foot Reset

2–3 sets × 10–15 reps

Purpose

Supports circulation and reduces lower-leg heaviness from long sitting periods.

How To Do It
  • Rise gently onto the balls of the feet.
  • Pause briefly.
  • Lower slowly.
  • Add toe raises if comfortable.
What You Should Feel

Light calf and shin effort with circulation increase.

Common Mistakes
  • Bouncing aggressively.
  • Ignoring cramp.
  • Locking knees.
  • Holding breath.
Easier / Harder

Easier: use support.
Harder: add pauses at the top.

Phase 3 — Reconnect & Downshift

Tai Chi Walking Pattern

2–5 minutes

Purpose

Encourages smoother movement rhythm and helps the nervous system shift away from prolonged stress mode.

How To Do It
  • Take slow deliberate steps.
  • Shift weight gradually.
  • Relax shoulders and jaw.
  • Keep breathing calm.
What You Should Feel

Calmer movement rhythm and reduced physical tension.

Common Mistakes
  • Rushing the steps.
  • Trying to move perfectly.
  • Holding tension.
  • Turning it into cardio.

Movement Linking

2–4 minutes

Purpose

Links movements together smoothly so the reset feels fluid rather than robotic.

How To Do It
  • Choose several movements from this page.
  • Move calmly between them.
  • Keep transitions smooth.
  • Stop before fatigue rises sharply.
What You Should Feel

Looser movement, calmer breathing and reduced stiffness.

Common Mistakes
  • Rushing transitions.
  • Turning it into a workout.
  • Forcing mobility.
  • Ignoring discomfort signals.

Final Posture Check

30–60 seconds

Purpose

Helps finish the reset feeling more open, grounded and connected to posture rather than collapsing straight back into sitting tension.

How To Do It
  • Stand tall but relaxed.
  • Keep knees soft.
  • Relax shoulders naturally.
  • Take several calm breaths.
What You Should Feel

Less compressed, less stiff and more physically awake.

Common Mistakes
  • Forcing rigid posture.
  • Tensing the lower back.
  • Holding breath.
  • Rushing back to sitting immediately.

Deskbound Recovery Principles

Helpful Habits

  • Frequent movement breaks
  • Changing posture regularly
  • Walking during calls where possible
  • Hydration and sleep support

Often Less Helpful

  • Sitting for many hours uninterrupted
  • Trying to “fix posture” through tension
  • Ignoring stiffness until severe
  • Replacing movement with more screen time
