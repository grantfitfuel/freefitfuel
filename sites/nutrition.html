Nutrition basics that still matter

Protein

Base most meals around a clear protein source to support muscle repair, satiety and recovery.

Carbs

Use carbohydrates to fuel training, walking, running and daily energy rather than fearing them automatically.

Fats

Include useful fats, but measure calorie-dense additions if weight loss is the current phase.

Consistency

The best plan is one you can repeat. Budget-friendly, familiar food is allowed.

The Core Four

Four everyday habits that make the biggest difference for health, energy and strength.

1. Balanced Nutrition

Use whole foods where possible: lean proteins, slow-digesting carbs, useful fats, fruit and veg.

2. Regular Movement

Strength work and aerobic movement support insulin sensitivity, heart health and everyday energy.

3. Sleep & Recovery

Recovery protects training quality. Food cannot fully compensate for consistently poor sleep.

4. Stress Management

Stress can affect appetite, energy and consistency. Use walks, gardening, stretching, yoga, tai chi, qi gong, art or photography as practical downshifts.

Injury Recovery & Physio

Supporting training also means adjusting nutrition and recovery when injury, pain or fatigue are present.

Common issues like plantar fasciitis, calf strains, tennis elbow or low back pain can derail progress. The Physio & Recovery page gives safe, practical progressions.

Educational only. If in doubt, seek professional review.
