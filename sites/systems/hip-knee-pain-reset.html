Hip & Knee Pain Reset

A fully coached lower-body recovery system for hip stiffness, knee irritation, walking discomfort, cramp and pain that worsens after inactivity.

Low-to-moderate intensity Walking support Movement restoration Recovery aware

Understanding The Pattern

Hip and knee pain often becomes worse when movement drops, stiffness builds up and the body loses confidence under load. Many people notice symptoms are strongest after sitting still, getting out of bed, restarting movement or after long inactive periods.

Important: Pain does not always mean damage is worsening. Stiff, sensitive or deconditioned tissues often become more reactive after inactivity. Gentle consistent movement is usually more helpful than complete avoidance.

Common Signs

  • Pain after sitting still
  • Hip stiffness when moving again
  • Walking discomfort
  • Deep aching around hip or knee
  • Cramp and tightness

Often Helpful

  • Frequent gentle movement
  • Gradual walking exposure
  • Hip and glute activation
  • Controlled loading
  • Mobility before long sitting periods

Seek Medical Advice If

  • Severe swelling develops
  • The joint locks repeatedly
  • You cannot weight-bear properly
  • Rapid weakness appears
  • Night pain becomes severe

How To Use This System

During Flare Periods

Use Phase 1 only. Keep movement calm, frequent and manageable.

When Symptoms Ease

Move into Phase 2 to rebuild lower-body confidence and walking tolerance.

When Stable

Use Phase 3 to return gradually to more normal training and activity.

Phase 1 — Restore Comfortable Movement

The goal is movement confidence. Gentle consistent movement usually beats complete inactivity for stiffness-driven pain.

Hip Circles

2 × 30–45 seconds

Purpose

Helps reduce hip stiffness and restore comfortable movement after sitting or inactivity.

Starting Position
  • Stand tall near a wall or chair for support.
  • Keep knees soft rather than locked.
  • Relax the shoulders and jaw.
How To Do It
  • Slowly circle the hips in one direction.
  • Move through a comfortable range only.
  • Reverse direction after 15–20 seconds.
  • Keep breathing relaxed throughout.
What You Should Feel

Gentle loosening around the hips and pelvis. You should not feel sharp pinching pain.

Common Mistakes
  • Moving too quickly.
  • Forcing painful range.
  • Locking the knees hard.
  • Holding the breath.
Easier / Harder

Easier: make the circles smaller.
Harder: increase the range slightly while staying comfortable.

Supported Sit-to-Stand

2–3 sets × 6–10 reps

Purpose

Rebuilds confidence standing up from seated positions and improves hip and knee loading tolerance.

Starting Position
  • Sit on a stable chair or bench.
  • Feet flat and roughly hip-width apart.
  • Use arm support if necessary.
How To Do It
  • Lean slightly forwards.
  • Push through the floor smoothly.
  • Stand without jerking upwards.
  • Lower slowly back to the chair.
What You Should Feel

Controlled effort through the hips, thighs and glutes rather than sharp knee pain.

Common Mistakes
  • Dropping quickly into the chair.
  • Using momentum to stand.
  • Knees collapsing inward aggressively.
  • Holding the breath.
Easier / Harder

Easier: use a higher chair.
Harder: slow the lowering phase to 3 seconds.

Gentle Walking Blocks

5–10 minutes

Purpose

Walking helps restore circulation, joint confidence and lower-body rhythm without excessive complexity.

How To Do It
  • Walk at a calm manageable pace.
  • Use shorter bouts if symptoms flare easily.
  • Keep steps relaxed rather than forceful.
  • Stop before symptoms spike sharply.
Walking Progression Rule

Increase either time or distance gradually, not both aggressively at once. Consistency matters more than speed.

Important Guidance

Some stiffness when restarting movement can improve once circulation increases. Monitor how you feel later that day and the next morning.

Common Mistakes
  • Trying to “walk it off” aggressively.
  • Large sudden step-count increases.
  • Completely avoiding walking for days.
  • Ignoring next-day symptom spikes.
Easier / Harder

Easier: split into several small walks.
Harder: gradually increase duration before pace.

Phase 2 — Build Lower-Body Capacity

Step-Ups

2–3 sets × 6–10 each side

Purpose

Builds hip, knee and ankle coordination while improving confidence during stairs and walking.

Starting Position
  • Use a low step initially.
  • Keep the whole foot on the step.
  • Use wall support if necessary.
How To Do It
  • Push through the stepping leg.
  • Stand smoothly onto the step.
  • Lower under control.
  • Avoid dropping heavily.
What You Should Feel

Hip and thigh effort with controlled balance work. Sharp joint pain is not the target.

Common Mistakes
  • Using a step that is too high.
  • Dropping quickly on descent.
  • Knee collapsing inward excessively.
  • Rushing the movement.
Easier / Harder

Easier: reduce step height.
Harder: slow the lowering phase or add light load.

Bridge Variations

2–3 sets × 8–12 reps

Purpose

Improves glute contribution so hips and knees share load more effectively.

Starting Position
  • Lie on your back with knees bent.
  • Feet flat and hip-width apart.
  • Arms relaxed by your sides.
How To Do It
  • Drive gently through the heels.
  • Lift the hips smoothly.
  • Pause briefly at the top.
  • Lower under control.
What You Should Feel

Glute and hip work more than lower-back strain.

Common Mistakes
  • Overarching the lower back.
  • Pushing through the toes only.
  • Rushing the lowering phase.
  • Holding excessive tension in the neck.
Easier / Harder

Easier: reduce height.
Harder: add a longer pause at the top.

Calf Raises

2–3 sets × 12–15 reps

Purpose

Builds lower-leg support for walking, balance and general movement confidence.

How To Do It
  • Hold a wall or chair if necessary.
  • Rise slowly onto the balls of the feet.
  • Pause briefly.
  • Lower under control.
What You Should Feel

Calf effort without cramping aggressively.

Cramp Guidance

If cramp appears repeatedly, reduce intensity, hydrate well and shorten the set temporarily.

Common Mistakes
  • Bouncing quickly.
  • Using momentum.
  • Holding the breath.
  • Ignoring cramp escalation.
Easier / Harder

Easier: use both hands for support.
Harder: increase pause time at the top.

Phase 3 — Return To More Normal Training

Controlled Squat Patterns

  • Use manageable depth.
  • Progress range gradually.
  • Keep the movement smooth and controlled.
  • Monitor next-day response.

Walking Tolerance Progression

  • Add distance gradually.
  • Do not spike activity suddenly.
  • Build consistency before intensity.
  • Use recovery walks on flare days.

Light Power Reintroduction

  • Mini pogo hops
  • Fast marching
  • Low-level dynamic movement
  • Only if symptoms remain stable
