Quick Resets (5–10 min)

Desk Decompress ~6–8 min

  1. Box Breathing — 4–4–4–4 for 12 cycles.
  2. Seated neck & shoulder circles — 2×20s/side.
  3. Standing Forward Fold — soft knees 60–90s.

Exhale slightly longer than inhale; jaw and tongue soft.

Floor Unknot ~8–10 min

  1. Constructive rest — 2–3 min.
  2. Reclined twist — 60–90s/side.
  3. Crocodile breathing — low belly breaths 3–4 min.

Evening Unwind (20–30 min)

Gentle Yin Flow ~25 min

  1. Child’s Pose (bolstered) — 3 min
  2. Cat–Cow — 1–2 min
  3. Low Lunge (blocks) — 2 min/side
  4. Wide-leg seated fold — 3–4 min
  5. Supported Bridge — 3 min
  6. Reclined Twist — 2 min/side
  7. Legs-Up-the-Wall — 5–7 min
  8. Savasana — 2–3 min

Breath-Led Unwind ~20–22 min

  1. 4-7-8 breathing — 10 cycles
  2. Supported forward fold — 3 min
  3. Supine hamstring (strap) — 2 min/side
  4. Box breathing — 6–8 cycles
  5. Body-scan in Savasana — 5–6 min

Read-Along Scripts (for recording or self-guided)

“Heavy Blanket” (5–7 min)

[Settle] Lie comfortably. Imagine a warm, heavy blanket being placed over you… from shoulders… to hips… to feet. Each time you breathe out, the blanket gets 5% heavier, 5% warmer. Jaw loosens. Eyes soften. Breath quiet.

[Scan] Feel the blanket over your chest… ribs… belly. On each exhale, the muscles below it switch off—the way a light goes out in a quiet house. Shoulders dim… arms dim… hands soft and still.

[Close] Stay here, or let the blanket ‘lift’ gently. Wiggle fingers, toes. Roll to one side and sit slowly.

“Forest Path” (8–10 min)

[Arrival] In your mind’s eye: a soft path under tall trees. Light filters in green and gold. Each step is slow. Each step is quiet.

[Detail] Notice the small things: a leaf’s edge, the weave of bark, a beam of dust-motes moving like snow. Your breath matches the rhythm of your walk: in… and… out… easy, even, unforced.

[Close] At the clearing, sit a moment. Soak in the peace. When you’re ready, follow the path back. Bring that quiet with you.

Why these help

  • Longer exhales nudge the parasympathetic system (rest/digest).
  • Imagery narrows attention and down-shifts threat detection.
  • Supported positions reduce muscular effort → brain reads “safe”.

Important Note

These practices support relaxation and general wellbeing; they are not a substitute for medical care. If you have health conditions, injuries, or mental health concerns, consult a qualified professional. Stop any practice that causes pain, dizziness, or distress.
