Knee Capacity Reset

A practical knee-control system for rebuilding confidence, strength and tolerance without turning every session into punishment. Use it as a standalone routine, or let My Plan draw from it when knee pain, stiffness, stairs, squats or return-to-training support are selected.

Start the reset Back to My Plan Recovery Hub
Time18 minutes
Frequency3–5 times weekly
IntensityLow to moderate
EquipmentChair, wall, step

What this is for

  • Knees that feel stiff after sitting.
  • Knees that feel uncertain on stairs.
  • Mild discomfort during squats, lunges or lower-body training.
  • Building tolerance before returning to heavier workouts.

Do not push through these

  • Sudden severe pain, major swelling, redness or heat.
  • Locking, giving way, numbness or unexplained weakness.
  • Recent fall, trauma or suspected ligament injury.
  • Pain that worsens rapidly or changes your normal walking.
Safety rule: keep pain around 0–3 out of 10. Stop if pain spikes, swelling appears, or symptoms are worse the next day.

Phase 1: Settle and Control

Use this in week 1 or whenever symptoms are flared. The aim is to calm the system and restore confidence.

Pain target: 0–3/10

Seated Knee Extensions

2–3 sets8–12 each side2 sec up / 2 sec down
Purpose

Wakes up the quadriceps and restores controlled knee movement without loading the joint heavily.

Starting position
  • Sit tall on a chair.
  • Both feet flat to begin.
  • Hands can rest lightly on the chair.
How to do it
  • Straighten one knee smoothly.
  • Pause briefly at the top.
  • Lower slowly instead of dropping.
  • Repeat on the other side.
What to feel

Light to moderate quad effort at the front of the thigh. Not sharp pain inside the knee.

Common mistakes
  • Snapping the knee straight.
  • Leaning back heavily.
  • Holding your breath.
  • Forcing through painful range.
Easier / harder

Easier: smaller range.
Harder: add a 2-second hold at the top.

Wall-Supported Sit-to-Stand

2–3 sets6–10 repsSlow lower / steady stand
Purpose

Builds confidence in the everyday movement of standing from a chair while teaching hip and knee control.

Starting position
  • Sit on a stable chair.
  • Feet flat and hip-width apart.
  • Knees roughly tracking over toes.
  • Use wall or hand support if needed.
How to do it
  • Lean slightly forwards.
  • Push through the floor.
  • Stand smoothly.
  • Lower back slowly without dropping.
What to feel

Thigh and glute effort. The knee should feel steadier, not more threatened.

Common mistakes
  • Dropping into the chair.
  • Knees collapsing inward.
  • Using momentum to launch up.
  • Choosing a chair that is too low too soon.
Easier / harder

Easier: use a higher chair.
Harder: lower for 3 seconds.

Standing Calf Raises

2 sets10–15 repsSmooth lift and lower
Purpose

Builds ankle and calf support, which helps walking, stairs and knee loading feel more stable.

Starting position
  • Stand tall near a wall or chair.
  • Feet about hip-width apart.
  • Knees soft rather than locked.
How to do it
  • Rise through the big-toe side of the foot.
  • Pause briefly at the top.
  • Lower slowly.
  • Avoid bouncing.
What to feel

Calf effort and foot control. Stop if cramp escalates or knee pain spikes.

Common mistakes
  • Rolling ankles outward.
  • Bouncing quickly.
  • Locking the knees hard.
  • Using no support when balance is poor.
Easier / harder

Easier: use both hands for support.
Harder: add a 2-second pause at the top.

Phase 2: Capacity Build

Use this when Phase 1 feels comfortable and symptoms return to baseline within 24 hours.

Weeks 2–4

Step-Up Control

3 sets6–10 each sideControlled up and down
Purpose

Improves stair confidence and teaches the hip, knee and ankle to share load.

Starting position
  • Use a low step or stair.
  • Place the whole foot on the step.
  • Hold support if needed.
How to do it
  • Drive through the mid-foot.
  • Stand onto the step smoothly.
  • Lower slowly.
  • Keep knee aligned with toes.
What to feel

Thigh, glute and calf effort with controlled balance. Not sharp joint pain.

Common mistakes
  • Using a step that is too high.
  • Letting the knee cave inward.
  • Dropping down quickly.
  • Pushing mainly from the trailing leg.
Easier / harder

Easier: lower step or more support.
Harder: 3-second descent.

Supported Split Squat Hold

2–3 sets15–30 sec each sideStatic hold
Purpose

Builds tolerance in a lunge-like position without needing repeated reps during early rebuilding.

Starting position
  • One foot forward and one foot back.
  • Hold a wall or chair.
  • Keep torso tall.
How to do it
  • Bend both knees slightly.
  • Hold a tolerable middle range.
  • Keep front foot grounded.
  • Breathe steadily.
What to feel

Muscular effort through thigh and glute. The knee should not feel like it is being stabbed or twisted.

Common mistakes
  • Dropping too low.
  • Leaning heavily forward.
  • Letting the front heel lift.
  • Holding through sharp pain.
Easier / harder

Easier: reduce depth.
Harder: add small controlled pulses.

Glute Bridge

3 sets8–12 reps2 sec up / 2 sec down
Purpose

Improves hip contribution so the knee is not asked to do all the work during lower-body movement.

Starting position
  • Lie on your back.
  • Knees bent, feet hip-width.
  • Ribs relaxed and arms by your side.
How to do it
  • Drive gently through heels.
  • Squeeze glutes to lift hips.
  • Pause briefly at the top.
  • Lower slowly.
What to feel

Glutes and back of hips. Not lower-back pinching.

Common mistakes
  • Overarching the lower back.
  • Pushing through toes only.
  • Rushing reps.
  • Letting knees collapse inward.
Easier / harder

Easier: reduce height.
Harder: 2-second hold at the top.

Phase 3: Return to Training

Use this when symptoms are stable and you are preparing for squats, lunges, stairs, walking volume and lower-body workouts.

Weeks 4+

Goblet Box Squat

3 sets6–10 reps3 sec down / steady up
Purpose

Rebuilds squat tolerance with a target depth and controlled lowering.

Starting position
  • Stand in front of a chair or box.
  • Feet comfortable and grounded.
  • Hold light dumbbell only if ready.
How to do it
  • Sit back under control.
  • Touch the chair lightly.
  • Stand smoothly.
  • Keep knees tracking over toes.
Common mistakes
  • Collapsing into the chair.
  • Choosing too much depth.
  • Letting knees cave inward.
  • Adding load before control is clean.
Easier / harder

Easier: bodyweight and higher chair.
Harder: light dumbbell and slower lowering.

Reverse Lunge to Support

2–3 sets6–8 each sideControlled step back
Purpose

Builds single-leg confidence and prepares for lunges without forcing unsupported depth too early.

How to do it
  • Stand beside support.
  • Step back softly.
  • Lower only as far as control allows.
  • Drive through the front foot to return.
What to feel

Front-leg control and glute/thigh effort. Not twisting knee pain.

Common mistakes
  • Step too long too soon.
  • Dropping into the bottom.
  • Front heel lifting.
  • Knee wobbling inward.
Easier / harder

Easier: shorter step.
Harder: less hand support or light load.

Low Pogo Preparation

2 sets10–20 secLight spring
Purpose

Prepares the knee and lower leg for impact gradually, only when symptoms are stable.

How to do it
  • Stand tall with soft knees.
  • Make tiny quiet bounces.
  • Use ankles more than deep knee bending.
  • Stop if pain spikes.
What to feel

Light spring through calves and feet. No jarring knee pain.

Easier / harder

Easier: march in place.
Harder: build to 30 seconds only if completely comfortable.

Progression rule

Progress only when pain stays at 0–3 out of 10 during movement and returns to baseline within 24 hours. If symptoms are worse the next day, drop back one level and reduce volume.

Good sign: the knee feels warmer, steadier or more confident after the session without next-day irritation.
